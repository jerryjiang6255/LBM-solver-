Educational use only. This simulator demonstrates the power of the Lattice Boltzmann Method (LBM) as an alternative to traditional Navier-Stokes CFD solvers. Unlike conventional CFD, LBM models fluid behaviour through particle distribution functions on a discrete lattice — collision and streaming steps replace the complex pressure-velocity coupling of Navier-Stokes. This makes LBM naturally parallel, highly efficient, and remarkably capable of handling complex geometries without the need for mesh generation or remeshing — as demonstrated by the sandbox drawing feature, where arbitrary walls can be placed in real time and the flow adapts instantly without any restart or remesh. The solver here uses a D2Q9 lattice with selectable collision / LES physics, pulsing inlet forcing, BFL interpolated boundary conditions, monitor plotting, and SDF-based geometry.
